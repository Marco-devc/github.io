Agenda 2030 Goals Related to Computer Science and Technology

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development is a global plan by the United Nations that includes 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Several of these goals are closely connected to computer science, technology companies, and computer components:

Goal 4 – Quality Education

Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all. Technology enables e-learning platforms, digital classrooms, and access to knowledge worldwide.

Goal 7 – Affordable and Clean Energy

Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all. Tech companies use clean energy in data centers and design energy-efficient hardware.

Goal 8 – Decent Work and Economic Growth>

Promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all. The tech industry generates jobs in programming, IT, cybersecurity, and remote work.

Goal 9 – Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure

Build resilient infrastructure, promote sustainable industrialization, and foster innovation. Computer science drives innovation in AI, robotics, smart systems, and digital infrastructure.

Goal 12 – Responsible Consumption and Production

Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns. Tech companies are encouraged to reduce e-waste, design eco-friendly devices, and promote recycling.

Goal 13 – Climate Action

Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts. Technology helps monitor climate change through AI, satellite data, and environmental sensors.
